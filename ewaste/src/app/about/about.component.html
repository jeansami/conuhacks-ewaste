About E waste

We are a generation obsessed with technology. Everywhere we look, technology is impacting our lives and assisting us with our daily routines. We have reached a point where more than 5 billion people now have a mobile phone with a connection. In North America and many parts of the world the average user purchases a new cell phone every 2 years and completely disregard their still functioning device. Worse still is when people throw away their used electronic devices without properly knowing where they should be throwing them. There are approximately 350,000 mobile phones being disposed of each day and very few of them are being recycled properly. Cellphones and other electronic devices that we use every day are made of precious metals which come from the Earth and are in fact finite. For every one million cell phones that are recycled, the EPA states that 35,274 pounds of copper, 772 pounds of silver, 75 pounds of gold and 33 pounds of palladium can be recovered.

Electronic waste is a problem that needs to be taken care of as the number of users of electronic devices grows exponentially every day. Not only can we recycle our old electronics, we can also repair them and keep them in use. Most of these electronics are actually still functioning or minimally damaged which can be easily repaired if given the right tools.
